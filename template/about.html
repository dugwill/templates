Welcome to the Comcast SCTE-35 Signal and Video Alignment Monitor.
This is a proof of concept tool. It should not be considered stable
and is subject to frequent outages for updates and testing.

What you will see on this tool is the SCTE-35 signals and video frames from the network feeds
with an indication of where the splice should take place (according to the SCTE-35 signal).
The system creates 4-5 splice points for each break. The first point (listed at type 54) is the
point were the splicer will begin to insert ad content.
The other splice points (listed as Type 55) come at 30, 60, 90 and 120 seconds after the type 54.
(The signal at 30s is rare). These are oppurtunites for the splicer to cleanly switch back to the
network feed. For a scheduled one minute break, the splicer will switch at the Type 54 and first type 55.
There is no ad insertion in this tool so you will not see ad content.
The times for the SCTE-35 signals are in the form of "Mon_Day_hhmmss.ss" and all times are GMT.

As it was inteneded to support the VDE Project, the monitoring tool is only looking at the output of Pillar,
the IP stream packager. This is after VACT makes any alignment changes, so those changes should be reflected here.

There are only 59 streams that are enabled for ad insertion via Pillar
(~36 networks, with East and West Coast feeds). So the scope is limited for now.
I am exploring the addition of mulitcast support in a future release.

This is a prototype tool and should not be used as a final determination of ad alignment.
It should only be used as a guide for taking a closer look at a particular stream.
